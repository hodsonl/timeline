1991.

Little to no design opportunities and user interaction, simple formatting available and with internet speeds so slow, large images were unadvised. View a few examples here.

1994.

The introduction of MySQL and PHP allowed user interaction and more complex websites, displaying information stored in databases which could come from the website itself. The term dynamic website was born.

1996.

Cascading Style Sheets are introduced and designers have a lot more freedom, designers begin to use as many new things as they can leaving websites cluttered and colourful.

2000.

Broadband internet is intorduced and internet speeds are increased up to twenty times, allowing designers to use larger images, animations and music that previusly would have taken too long to load.

2002.

The term SmartPhone starts to popularise and mobile browsing begind its life. Website designers begin to take note that this may turn into something in the future as 3G has just been released.

2007.

The iPhone is released and companies need to make their website designers need to compensate for the massive increase in mobile browsing. Responive design and mobile websites become some what of a norm.

2008.

Google Chrome is launched and cross-browser support begins to become a major aspect of designing a website.

2011.

CSS3 is introduced and brings along a variety of new features for designers, including media queries, gradients and much more.

2004.

As template websites begin to build momentum, website designers move towards offering uniqueness to compensate. Grid designs take popularity too.

2017.

Website designers now have to take into account users browsing their websites on augmented reality glasses. Using media queries or a wearable computing version of their site are the suggested methods.

2012.

A trend of big elements on a page occurs, big images, text, headers and footers. Simplicity is the key to a clean looking website.

2019.

Web 3.0 changes website design, designers now have have access to a lot more information from across a vast number of databases. Front end design compensates for this change.

1and1.

Website design template websites begin to build popularity, customers can build their own website for a cheaper price than a custom website designer.
